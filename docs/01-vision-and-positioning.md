# 01 — Vision and positioning

## One line
An agentic-AI assistant that does ~95% of a geotechnical engineer's design work, step by step, while the engineer stays the expert of record — leading, checking, and approving every step.

## Who it is for
- **The individual engineer**, in any company — not enterprise/firm software.
- Think of it like a calculator or a very capable assistant that accelerates the design procedure for one engineer at a time.

## What it is NOT
- **Not an advanced numerical analyzer** (not a PLAXIS / FEM / FDM replacement).
- **Not an enterprise platform** bought by a firm and rolled out top-down.
- It replicates and accelerates **what an engineer actually does** by hand / with standard methods — interpretation, parameter selection, code checks, calculation setup, and drafting.

## Value proposition
- AI performs ~95% of the procedural work; the engineer leads, checks, and confirms.
- The engineer remains **accountable and responsible** — the expert of record. Every step is reviewable, overridable, and signed off (see `03`).
- Productivity multiplier for the engineer, not a black box.

## Standards / codes
- **Code-agnostic by design.** The engineer selects their standard/code **first**; that choice drives factors, methods, and required checks.
- **Eurocode 7 is the reference implementation, not the limit.** Reference texts in `../Sources/`:
  - `EN 1997-1:2004` — Part 1, general design rules.
  - `EN 1997-2:2007` — Part 2, ground investigation and testing.
- Other codes (AASHTO/FHWA, BS, IS, AS, local national annexes, etc.) are first-class once added.

## Market
- Launch focus: **Europe and Australia** first.
